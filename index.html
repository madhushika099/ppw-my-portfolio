Reflective Journal

My learning journey in preparing for the professional world.

Journal Entry 1: Introduction to PPW
What I LearnedPPW is a compulsory module focused on professional preparation. Key assessments include a quiz, portfolio, and interview role play.
How I Will Apply ThisI will build my portfolio carefully, ensure all sections are complete, improve my CV, and include relevant certificates as evidence.
My ReflectionProfessional preparation must start before graduation. Technical knowledge alone is not enough.
What I Want to ImproveI want to improve how I present myself through my portfolio, CV, and interviews, and set clearer career goals.
Journal Entry 2: Presentation Skills
What I LearnedPresentations need a strong closing. Slides with too much text weaken delivery, and the presenter should remain the focus.
How I Will Apply ThisI will use simple slides with key points only, match my spoken explanation with the slides, and end with a proper conclusion and Q&A slide.
My ReflectionAudiences remember how ideas are delivered. Clear explanation and confident delivery matter more than slide design.
What I Want to ImproveI want to improve my confidence and voice clarity by practicing before every presentation.
Journal Entry 3: Business Writing and Formal Letter Writing
What I LearnedProfessional writing must be clear, concise, polite, and formal. Formal letters follow a fixed structure.
How I Will Apply ThisI will use "Yours faithfully" when the recipient's name is unknown and "Yours sincerely" when the recipient is known.
My ReflectionProfessional writing is about clarity and respect, not just grammar. A well-written letter creates a positive impression.
What I Want to ImproveI want to practice request, complaint, and application letters and strengthen proofreading before submission.
Journal Entry 4: Email Writing and Memo Writing
What I LearnedEmails are for external communication, while memos are for internal use. Resignation letters must maintain a positive tone.
How I Will Apply ThisI will write professional emails with clarity and politeness and offer support during handover when needed.
My ReflectionProfessional writing matters even after joining a workplace. How someone leaves a job reflects their character.
What I Want to ImproveI want to improve workplace emails, memos, and formal letters with the correct tone and structure.
Journal Entry 5: Telephone Etiquette
What I LearnedTelephone communication relies on speaking and listening. A good call needs clear speech, active listening, proper timing, and polite interaction.
How I Will Apply ThisI will prepare key points before calling, choose the right time, introduce myself clearly, and listen carefully.
My ReflectionWithout body language, tone and voice clarity become critical. A poorly handled call can leave a negative impression.
What I Want to ImproveI want to build confidence in professional calls through telephone role plays and proper preparation.
Journal Entry 6: Report Writing
What I LearnedReports record evidence and decisions for future reference. They must be audience-focused with relevant content only.
How I Will Apply ThisI will write structured reports, cite sources in IEEE format, and place large data in appendices.
My ReflectionA good report is organized, neutral, and evidence-based. Personal opinions should only appear when they support the purpose.
What I Want to ImproveI want to improve report structure, IEEE referencing accuracy, and appendix usage.
Journal Entry 7: Interview Preparation and CV Writing
What I LearnedA CV must be clean and professional. IT companies prefer PDF format, and LinkedIn and GitHub profiles are important additions.
How I Will Apply ThisI will refine my CV, export it as PDF, update LinkedIn, and organize GitHub to demonstrate practical work.
My ReflectionA CV is the first impression I make on a recruiter. Small formatting decisions matter.
What I Want to ImproveI want a stronger CV, LinkedIn profile, and GitHub profile that support job applications effectively.
Journal Entry 8: Presentation Skills Advanced
What I LearnedEffective delivery involves vocal variety, eye contact, body language, posture, stage movement, and timing.
How I Will Apply ThisI will practice before presentations, keep slides clean, and coordinate transitions in group presentations.
My ReflectionA successful presentation depends on both preparation and delivery. Confidence comes from practice.
What I Want to ImproveI want to improve eye contact, voice control, body language, and group presentation timing.
